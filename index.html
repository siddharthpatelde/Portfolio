More about me

Greetings! My name is Siddharth, and I am thrilled to introduce myself as an aspiring engineer with a passion for robotics. Determined to turn my dreams into reality, I chose Germany as the perfect destination to cultivate my engineering skills. With its renowned reputation for fostering innovation and technological advancement, Germany is undeniably the best place for engineers like me to thrive.

My journey towards becoming a robotics expert began with an exciting decision to study the German language intensively. Today, I can proudly say that I have achieved a C1 level proficiency, which has enabled me to seamlessly integrate into the German culture and academic environment. Soon, I will embark on my Bachelor's program at HTW Berlin University of Applied Sciences, where I aim to specialize in Mechatronics and Electrotechnik.

Though my official engineering education is yet to commence, I have already acquired substantial knowledge in my field. Through countless personal projects, I have honed my skills in creating embedded systems tailored to meet specific client requirements. These projects have allowed me to explore my creativity, solve real-world challenges, and gain invaluable experience.

I believe in learning by doing, and my experiences have nurtured a deep understanding of Mechatronics and Electrotechnik. My enthusiasm for robotics has led me to develop various innovative projects, some of which include Holographic effects, RFID systems, Mars rover prototype. Each project has taught me valuable lessons and provided me with a solid foundation to excel in my future studies and career.

With a boundless determination and an unyielding passion for robotics, I look forward to immersing myself in the vibrant academic atmosphere at German University. My ultimate goal is to become a prominent figure in the field of robotics and contribute to the advancement of automation technology.

Overview of some of my work

Velocity control of a DC brushed motor using SOLO UNO V2

Developed a small Arduino code and created a circuit for controlling the velocity of a DC brushed motor without any physical feedback sensor (such as encoders), solely using an observer.

work-in-progress
Velocity control of a DC brushed geared motor using encoder feedback with Kangaroo X2.

Developed a circuit to control the speed/velocity of the motor using Sabertooth 2x12 and Kangaroo X2 (Dimension Engineering products).

work-in-progress
Developed a combined circuit for controlling the RPM of both a DC brushed motor with an observer and a geared motor with an encoder.

Developed a circuit for a student working on their bachelor's final thesis, involving speed control for both motors.

work-in-progress
A simple Arduino-based PI algorithm to control the speed/velocity of the motor.

I worked on applying a PI control loop to regulate the speed of a DC geared motor solely using an encoder as a feedback sensor and a microcontroller, without relying on any external modules.

work-in-progress
Study of magnetic encoders for speed control of motors.

In order to develop any feedback control loop for regulating speed or position, it's essential to utilize feedback from a sensor. I opted to use encoders for this purpose.
